The Little Prince

As the little prince drifted into sleep, I gently carried him in my arms and walked on. I felt a deep emotion stirring within me, as though I were holding something delicate and precious. It seemed to me that nothing on Earth could be more fragile.

Bathed in moonlight, I gazed at his face—his closed eyes, his soft hair quivering in the breeze—and thought to myself: “What I see is only a shell. What truly matters lies within, unseen.”

When his lips curved into the faintest smile, I reflected again: “What touches me so profoundly is this child’s devotion to his flower—the vision of a rose illuminating his life, glowing like the flame of a lamp, even in his sleep.”

And in that moment, I felt an even greater tenderness, as though he were a flame himself, one that could be extinguished by the gentlest breath of wind.
